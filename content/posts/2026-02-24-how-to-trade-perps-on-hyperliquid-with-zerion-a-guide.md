With Zerion, you can do all from the same wallet you already use for swaps and portfolio tracking.</p><p>This guide covers what perps are, how to place your first trade, and what to watch out for along the way.</p><h2 id="what-are-perpetual-futures"><strong>What are perpetual futures?</strong></h2><p>Perps are derivative contracts that let you speculate on an asset's price without owning it.&nbsp;</p><p>Unlike traditional futures, they have no expiry date. You can hold a position as long as your account has enough margin to support it.</p><p>Two core mechanics make perps work:</p><ul><li><strong>Long and short positions.</strong> Go long if you expect the price to rise, short if you expect it to fall.</li><li><strong>Leverage.</strong> Amplify your exposure relative to your deposit. With 10x leverage, a $1,000 deposit controls a $10,000 position. This cuts both ways as gains and losses are both magnified.</li></ul><p>Perps use a funding rate mechanism to keep their price close to the spot market. Funding payments are exchanged between long and short traders periodically. They affect your overall PnL while you hold a position: you either pay funding rate or earn it.</p><h2 id="how-to-trade-perps-on-zerion">How to trade perps on Zerion</h2><p>Perps are now live in Zerion! You can add or remove margin to adjust your liquidation price.</li><li><strong>Liquidation price</strong> — The price at which your position is automatically closed to prevent further losses. This shifts when you change your margin or position size.</li><li><strong>PnL (Profit and Loss)</strong> — Your unrealized or realized gain or loss, based on the difference between your entry price and the current market price.</li><li><strong>Funding payments</strong> — Periodic payments exchanged between long and short holders to keep perp prices aligned with the spot market.</li><li><strong>Size</strong> — The total value of your position, including leverage.</li></ul><h2 id="managing-risk-with-leverage">Managing risk with leverage</h2><p>Leverage amplifies everything.&nbsp;</p><p>A 10x long position gains 10% when the underlying asset moves 1% in your favor. But it also loses 10% on a 1% move against you.</p><p>A few practical guidelines:</p><ul><li><strong>Start with lower leverage.</strong> Even 2x–5x adds meaningful exposure without putting your margin at immediate risk.</li><li><strong>Always set a stop loss.</strong> Markets move fast, especially in crypto. A stop loss gives you a defined maximum downside.</li><li><strong>Watch your liquidation price.</strong> If the market approaches it, you can add margin to push it further away—or close the position entirely.</li></ul><h2 id="fees">Fees</h2><p>Zerion Perps runs on <a href="https://hyperliquid.xyz/"><u>Hyperliquid</u></a>, the leading onchain perps DEX with deepest liquidity.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p><p>Each trade incurs a Zerion fee on top of Hyperliquid's 0.0432% base fee: 0.04% or 0.02% if you have <a href="https://zerion.io/premium"><u>Zerion Premium</u></a>.
