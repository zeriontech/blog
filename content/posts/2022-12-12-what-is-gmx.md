You don't actually buy or sell any token. Instead, you deposit collateral and can take long and short positions. At settlement, the profit is paid in USDC (for shorts) or the pair's other token (for longs).</p><p>On GMX, you get leverage of up to 50x. </p><p>Coupled with zero price impact trades, limit orders, and low swap fees, GMX became a magnet for degens. Especially those who try to qualify for potential Arbitrum airdrop.</p><p>The market prices are based on Chainlink's oracles, which aggregates price feeds from leading exchanges. This makes positions safe from liquidations due to random ticks on a single automated market maker.</p><p>As a result, GMX became the top dapp on Arbitrum by TVL <em>and </em>the leading perpetual exchange in DeFi. The total trading volume exceeded $80 billion, as of December 2022.</p><p>As a developer, you can also access GMX token and protocol positions for any address with Zerion's <a href="https://zerion.io/api/arbitrum" rel="noreferrer">Arbitrum API</a>.</p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card kg-card-hascaption"><img src="https://images.surferseo.art/52a65f69-123f-41b3-9c74-4fefca014322.png" class="kg-image" alt="GMX total value locked locked rose in the bear market" loading="lazy" width="2266" height="946"><figcaption><span style="white-space: pre-wrap;">GMX total value locked locked rose in the bear market. Source: </span><a href="https://defillama.com/protocol/gmx"><span style="white-space: pre-wrap;">DeFiLlama</span></a></figcaption></figure><h3 id="the-founding-team">The founding team</h3><p>GMX is founded by anons.</p><p>The protocol started as the merger of XVIX and Gambit, which converted their tokens to GMX. One public-facing devs is X.</p><p>While a fully anon team could be a risk, this also insulates the dapp from regulations and frivolous lawsuits.</p><h2 id="how-does-gmx-work">How does GMX work?</h2><p>GMX isn't the first decentralized derivatives market but it has many unique features that other projects lack.</p><p>The core feature of GMX is a community-operated ‘unionized' liquidity pool – the <strong>GLP pool</strong>. GLP is an index used to provide liquidity for leveraged trading. The GLP holders earn when traders lose (leverage makes it easy!) and vice versa.</p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card kg-card-hascaption"><img src="https://images.surferseo.art/2d8c66f5-55ca-4785-9942-ba0d87de7ade.png" class="kg-image" alt="Charts showing Traders Net PnL and Traders Profit vs Loss at GMX against GLP holders" loading="lazy" width="2778" height="954"><figcaption><span style="white-space: pre-wrap;">Traders have been losing against GLP holders. Source: </span><a href="https://stats.gmx.io/"><span style="white-space: pre-wrap;">GMX stats</span></a></figcaption></figure><p>Traders on GMX  benefit from zero price impact trades and pay two types of fees:</p><ul><li>Trading fee of 0.1% of the position size to open a trade</li><li>"Borrow fee" equal to <em>(assets borrowed)/(total assets in pool) * 0.01%</em> per hour</li></ul><p>This borrow fee depends on the utilization of the asset: it's lower for less popular assets and higher for more popular assets.</p><p>All platform fees are split between GMX and GLP token holders:</p><ul><li>30% to GMX stakers</li><li>70% to GLP holders, i.e. liquidity providers.</li></ul><h2 id="glp-the-liquidity-providers-token">GLP, the Liquidity Providers token</h2><p>GLP is a LP token for providing liquidity to traders.</p><p>You can buy GLP tokens with any of the asset that are in the liquidity pool: USDC, WETH, WBTC, DAI, etc.</p><p>The fee depends on what's in the liquidity pool. It's cheaper to buy GLP with index assets that are demanded by the market but are underrepresented in the pool.</p><p>GLP on Arbitrum and Avalanche are different tokens. They have different compositions, different prices, and and cannot bridged. </p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card kg-card-hascaption"><img src="https://images.surferseo.art/3c01a3e4-32a5-4a2e-b368-adca18e15e5e.png" class="kg-image" alt="GLP composition on Arbitrum and Avalanche" loading="lazy" width="2646" height="1118"><figcaption><span style="white-space: pre-wrap;">GLP composition. The total supply now is ~8.2 million tokens, 85% of those are staked.</p><h3 id="staking-gmx">Staking GMX</h3><p>Staked GMX tokens earn three types of rewards:</p><ul><li>Escrowed GMX (esGMX tokens), which come from liquidity incentives and vest gradually</li><li>Variable ETH and AVAX APR from the 30% of fees generated from leveraged trading</li><li>Multiplier points that boost APRs and encourage long-term staking</li></ul><h2 id="how-can-you-use-gmx">How can you use GMX?</h2><p>The GMX exchange is a viable Web3 alternative to centralized exchanges.
