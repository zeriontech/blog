<p>Polygon is one of the leading EVM networks and is home to many NFTs.</p><p>With a string of a-list partners and an ever-expanding ecosystem, Polygon is a network that’s worth paying attention to. However, Polygon NFT collections are relatively lesser-known compared to Ethereum, perhaps because many wallets don’t fully support them.</p><p>In this guide, we’ll dive into the Polygon blockchain, see where to buy Polygon NFTs and how to view them.</p><h2 id="understanding-the-polygon-blockchain">Understanding the Polygon blockchain</h2><p>Polygon is a whole set of technologies for scaling Ethereum.</p><p>The best-known <a href="https://zerion.io/blog/what-is-polygon-matic-and-how-to-use-it-with-zerion-wallet/">Polygon network</a> is the Polygon Proof of Stake network. It’s an EVM network, which means it works like Ethereum. Developers can easily port their Ethereum smart contracts to Polygon. And you can use the same wallets as for the mainnet Ethereum.</p><p>The main advantages of Polygon over Ethereum are lower fees and fast transaction finality.</p><p>Meanwhile, Polygon has a much broader ecosystem than other networks. Many exchanges, protocols, platforms, apps, and wallets support Polygon.</p><p>Finally, Polygon has built partnerships with some of the biggest brands: Starbucks, Draftkings, The Walt Disney Company, Adidas, Meta, Adobe, and many others.</p><h2 id="how-to-bridge-funds-to-polygon">How to bridge funds to Polygon</h2><p>Before you can buy your first Polygon NFT, you need to get MATIC.</p><p>While some NFT marketplaces support other assets such as USDC and WETH, MATIC is needed to pay gas for transactions.</p><p>You can do this with the <a href="https://app.zerion.io/bridge?utm_source=blog&amp;utm_medium=article&amp;utm_campaign=polygon_nfts">Zerion bridge</a> — it will find the cheapest and fastest Polygon bridge.
