You can also track all staked Polygon tokens with Zerion's <a href="https://zerion.io/api/polygon" rel="noreferrer">Polygon API</a>. </p><h2 id="how-matic-staking-works"><strong>How MATIC staking works</strong></h2><p>Polygon PoS (Proof of Stake) is a blockchain in which validators and delegators stake MATIC on the Ethereum mainnet to secure the Polygon network.</p><p>Anyone can stake MATIC but usually you need to connect to Polygon staking's website. You also need to manually select the validator. And finally, you lock up your MATIC tokens when you stake. If you suddenly need those tokens, you must wait for 3-4 days before they can be unstaked.</p><p>That's where Lido comes handy with its liquid staking solution.</p><h2 id="lido-staking"><strong>Lido Staking</strong></h2><p>Lido Finance is the leading <a href="https://zerion.io/blog/how-to-stake-eth-lido-vs-rocket-pool-vs-stakewise/">liquid staking</a> protocol <em>and </em>the biggest DeFi protocol by the total value locked.</p><p>Managed by Lido DAO, the protocol offers smart contracts for staking Ethereum, Matic, Solana, and other assets. Meanwhile, Lido's node operator partners take care of maintaining staking infrastructure.</p><p>Importantly, liquid staking gives you separate liquid tokens that represent your staked assets. For MATIC tokens, stMATIC is the wrapped version of MATIC staked in Lido. You can use stMATIC just like regular tokens: send to anyone, swap, or use in DeFi protocols.</p><p>As long as you hold stMATIC, it will earn staking rewards. However, to save on gas fees, these rewards are not paid out. Instead, the price of stMATIC goes up as the Lido staking pool accumulates daily rewards.</p><p>Now that you know how this works, let's see how to stake MATIC with Zerion.</p><h2 id="stake-matic-on-lido-with-zerion"><strong>Stake MATIC on Lido with Zerion</strong></h2><p>To begin, make sure you have MATIC on the Ethereum network.
