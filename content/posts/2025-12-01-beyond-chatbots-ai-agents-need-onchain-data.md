<p>AI agents have emerged as a full-blown new crypto category. Endlessly creating content, replying 24/7, and even trading tokens, AI agents are great at capturing attention. However, most agents today are still far from full autonomy. Access to onchain data is one of the major hurdles to achieving greater sentience and sovereignty. In this post, we’ll explore the present and the future of AI agents and how tools like Zerion API are paving the way for their evolution.</p><h2 id="ai-agents-today">AI agents today&nbsp;</h2><p>There are two opposing opinions about AI agents:</p><p>Some see AI agents as the future of blockchain—a new kind of user who will soon far outnumber humans using crypto. Agents can simplify onboarding for new users and make crypto more accessible to everybody, abstracting away blockchain complexities.</p><p>The skeptical view is that AI agents are just a glorified version of bots.</p><p>The truth is probably somewhere in between. AI agents are based on LLM models, and unlike bots, they don’t have pre-programmed behavior. However, they also don’t have full autonomy simply due to the way they are set up.&nbsp;</p><p>Most AI agents today are built using:</p><ul><li><strong>LLM models</strong> such as ChatGPT, Claude, Llama, or even several instances (infinite backrooms) as in the <strong>terminal of truths,</strong></li><li><strong>Data</strong> for training or tuning the model,&nbsp;</li><li><strong>Connectors</strong> for receiving prompts and sharing output via platforms like X, Discord, or Telegram,&nbsp;</li><li><strong>Memory</strong> for maintaining coherent personalities,&nbsp;</li><li>An <strong>ERC-20 token</strong> affiliated with the agent.</li></ul><p>Of course, the token is what most degens see and care about.&nbsp;</p><p>Yet the token isn’t the only onchain component. Some agents also control wallets and can make transactions. It is essentially another connector for sharing output.&nbsp;</p><p>For example, Farcaster-based AI agents like <a href="https://warpcast.com/clanker" rel="noreferrer"><strong>Clanker</strong></a> are experimenting with wallet interactions. Clanker launches new tokens, deploying smart contracts and liquidity pools. These agents showcase the potential for AI to directly manage onchain activity.&nbsp;</p><p>Other agents like <a href="https://www.freysa.ai/" rel="noreferrer"><strong>Freysa</strong></a> strive for sovereignty, where they have exclusive control over a wallet's private keys.&nbsp;</p><p>However, most popular AI agents today don’t yet see or use onchain data.</p><h2 id="onchain-ai-agents-of-tomorrow">Onchain AI agents of tomorrow&nbsp;</h2><p>In the future, we can expect AI agents to gain full access to onchain data.</p><p>But there’s a challenge: LLM-based AI agents operate in human language and cannot easily process raw onchain data in hexadecimal or bytes.
