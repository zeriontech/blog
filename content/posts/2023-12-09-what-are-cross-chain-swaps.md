<p>Gone are the days when all the action was happening just on the Ethereum mainnet.&nbsp;</p><p>With the proliferation of EVM networks, many interesting projects are launching straight on those chains to benefit from lower fees and faster transactions. So by now, you’re probably used to bridging.&nbsp;</p><p>Did you know you can also bridge and swap in the same transaction? That’s also known as cross-chain swaps. In this post, we’ll explain how cross chain swaps work and how they can help you move faster.&nbsp;</p><h2 id="the-basics-of-cross-chain-swaps">The basics of cross chain swaps</h2><p>Cross-chain swaps are onchain transactions in which you sell a token on one network and get another token on the other network.&nbsp;</p><p>This type of swap is crucial when you need to get a specific token on the destination network. For example, you might have only ETH but need some MATIC to pay for gas on Polygon. Or you might want to use USDC in some dapp on Arbitrum but have only DAI and USDT across various other chains.</p><p>With most bridges, you’d need to do 2 or even 3 transactions:&nbsp;</p><ul><li>Swap tokens on the origination network&nbsp;</li><li>Bridge tokens&nbsp;</li><li>Swap tokens on the destination network (if the bridge didn’t support the token you need)</li></ul><p>With cross-chain swaps, you can do that in one transaction!&nbsp;</p><p>You simply interact with a protocol, sending it a token. The protocol then sends you another token on a different network. This way, you manage to save both your time and gas costs.</p><h2 id="how-cross-cross-chain-swap-work">How cross cross-chain swap work</h2><p>For you, cross-chain swaps are not much different from using a decentralized exchange. Connect a wallet and sign transactions. Boom, it’s done, all in a self-custodial way.&nbsp;</p><p>However, the swap happens on two networks:&nbsp;</p><ul><li>On your original network (e.g., Optimism), you transfer a token to a smart contract</li><li>An address on the destination network (e.g., Base) sends another token to you.&nbsp;</li></ul><p>Depending on the speed of the networks, you might need to wait a few minutes or up to an hour for the second transaction to hit your wallet. (Don’t worry, it will get there!)</p><p>While only these ‘send’ and ‘receive’ will appear in your <a href="https://zerion.io/blog/multichain-transaction-history-in-one-feed/"><u>transaction history</u></a>, a lot of stuff happens in between.</p><p>Under the hood, cross-chain swaps use a combination of DEXes and bridges, connected through smart contracts and back-end engines. This aggregation work is done by interoperability protocols like <u>Socket</u>.&nbsp;</p><p>Socket has done the hard work of bringing together liquidity (i.e., money) and data from across 15 different bridges that serve the wider Ethereum ecosystem. Socket effectively lets other dapps build their own bridges and embed them with other services.&nbsp;</p><p>Socket powers cross-chain swaps for both Zerion and Matcha, finding the best routes for swapping and bridging in one go.</p><h3 id="swap-with-zerion">Swap with Zerion</h3><p>With Zerion, you can bridge and swap in the same transaction.&nbsp;</p><ol><li>Select the token that you want to sell and the network&nbsp;</li><li>Select the token that you want to get and the network where you want to</li><li>Sign transactions</li></ol><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><img src="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/2023/12/Screenshot-2023-12-10-at-21.54.53.png" class="kg-image" alt="" loading="lazy" width="1920" height="1228" srcset="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/size/w600/2023/12/Screenshot-2023-12-10-at-21.54.53.png 600w, https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/size/w1000/2023/12/Screenshot-2023-12-10-at-21.54.53.png 1000w, https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/size/w1600/2023/12/Screenshot-2023-12-10-at-21.54.53.png 1600w, https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/2023/12/Screenshot-2023-12-10-at-21.54.53.png 1920w" sizes="(min-width: 720px) 720px"></figure><p>You can do cross-chain swaps right in <a href="https://link.zerion.io/okiN8qwfUNb" rel="noreferrer">Zerion Wallet</a> on mobile.
