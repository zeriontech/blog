They will likely shill their bags <em>after they make trades</em>.&nbsp;</p><p>Also, don’t blindly follow public lists of hedge fund addresses or other well-known wallets. Too many people look at those, so there is probably little alpha.&nbsp;</p><p>If you don’t source your alpha, you might become someone else’s exit liquidity.&nbsp;</p><p>Instead, you can search onchain to find your own source of alpha — this post will show you how.&nbsp;</p><p>We’ll use 3 tools:&nbsp;</p><ul><li><a href="https://dexscreener.com/" rel="noreferrer">DEX Screener</a> (free)</li><li><a href="https://basescan.org/" rel="noreferrer">Basescan</a> or another explorer for other chains (free)</li><li><a href="https://link.zerion.io/mO9MIo4jyIb" rel="noreferrer">Zerion Wallet</a> and <a href="https://app.zerion.io/?utm_source=whale-trades&amp;utm_medium=blog" rel="noreferrer">Zerion web app</a> (free or $99 a year for Zerion Premium)</li></ul><p>You’ll find trending tokens, identify top traders, and get alerts when they do new trades.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p><h2 id="step-1-find-trending-tokens">Step 1: Find trending tokens</h2><p>Go on <a href="https://dexscreener.com/" rel="noreferrer"><strong>DEX Screener</strong></a> and select trending tokens.
