<p>You’ve seen people brag about big gains with Optimism’s and Arbitrum’s airdrops.</p><p>But dozens of other Web3 projects have also done airdrops. Consistently getting airdrops is a great way to build up a portfolio, even when starting with just a few hundred dollars.</p><p>In this post, we’ll show how to find and qualify for airdrops with just a smartphone and <a href="https://link.zerion.io/UWoa61g08zb" rel="noreferrer"><strong>Zerion Wallet</strong></a>.</p><h2 id="crypto-airdrop-basics">Crypto airdrop basics</h2><p>If you’ve already got some airdrops, you know how they work. Feel free to skip this.</p><p>If you’re new to this, here’s how you get crypto airdrops in general:</p><ol><li>You do some onchain actions (trade, mint an NFT, etc) on a new network, in a dapp or a protocol</li><li>The team behind the network/dapp/protocol takes a snapshot, i.e. it records all addresses that interacted with them onchain</li><li>This team cleans up that list, aiming to remove bots and abusers (aka Sybil attackers)</li><li>The team announces the airdrop and publishes the list of eligible addresses — the announcement date can be a few weeks or even months after the snapshot</li><li>Once the new token is live, you can claim it</li><li>That’s it! You can now sell the token on an exchange or hodl it.</li></ol><div class="kg-card kg-callout-card kg-callout-card-yellow"><div class="kg-callout-emoji">⚠️</div><div class="kg-callout-text"><b><strong style="white-space: pre-wrap;">Warning:</strong></b> scammers often create fake airdrop websites. Double-check the URL and social media accounts.</div></div><p>But the risks are worth it. Uniswap, one of the earliest DeFi airdrops, distributed at least 400 UNI (~$2,400) to every user, Optimism dropped over $2000 on average, and Arbitrum at least $700.</p><p>Many newbies wonder why Web3 projects “give out free money” via airdrops.</p><h2 id="why-projects-do-airdrops">Why projects do airdrops</h2><p>Web3 projects launch airdrops to decentralize.</p><p>If a protocol is controlled by a single organization or a few founders, it isn’t really decentralized. However, Web3 projects can launch governance tokens and eventually transfer control to token holders.</p><p>The two main ways to distribute tokens are token sales and airdrops.</p><p>Selling tokens immediately generates some revenue. But it also has legal risks: the SEC can treat tokens as securities and go after founders.</p><p>Airdrops don’t have that risk. Meanwhile, giving away free tokens doesn’t cost anything.</p><p>Both token sales and airdrops create new liquid markets into which founding teams and their investors can eventually sell their token allocations.</p><p>Of course there are many nuances, but it’s all you need to know.</p><p>It’s more important to understand the common types of crypto airdrops.</p><h2 id="l2-airdrops">L2 airdrops</h2><p>Layer 2 (L2) scaling solutions for Ethereum have done some of the biggest airdrops.</p><p>Optimism and Arbitrum are prime examples. Usually, addresses qualify for an airdrop if they have interacted with the projects onchain.</p><h3 id="how-do-i-get-new-crypto-airdrops">How do I get new crypto airdrops?</h3><p>To get retroactive airdrops, you must have done some onchain actions (trading, providing liquidity, etc) <em>before </em>the airdrop is announced. New crypto airdrops are not announced in advance. Instead, you need to start using dapps and protocols that do not yet have a token. In future, these projects might announce their native token and distribute it through an airdrop.</p><h3 id="how-much-does-crypto-airdrop-cost">How much does crypto airdrop cost?</h3><p>Retroactive airdrops are given away for free (although you need to pay gas to claim the airdrop). However, to qualify for airdrops you need to do some transactions onchain. These transactions cost gas but aren’t expensive compared to even the smallest airdrops.</p><h3 id="how-do-i-find-good-crypto-airdrops">How do I find good crypto airdrops?</h3><p>The best crypto airdrops are never announced in advanced. Instead, you should try using different layers, dapps, and protocols. Be an active Web3 citizen and it will pay off in the longer term.</p><h3 id="am-i-eligible-for-airdrops">Am I eligible for airdrops?</h3><p>You should only check your eligibility on the project’s official website. Never trust randon tweets or DMs, claiming that you are suddenly eligible for an airdrop. Those are most likely scams that will drain your wallet after you connect it to a fake airdrop website.</p><h3 id="are-crypto-airdrops-risky">Are crypto airdrops risky?</h3><p>The main risk in crypto airdrops is using a fake website to claim tokens. With real airdrops, there is never an urgency to connect your wallet and claim tokens. Always double check the URL and the messages you sign with your wallet.</p>
