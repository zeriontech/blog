This means that instead of miners (like in Bitcoin), validators add transactions to the blockchain, earning block rewards and transaction fees.&nbsp;</p><p>Anyone can become a validator by staking 32 ETH, which serves as a security guarantee. If a validator tries to cheat or otherwise breaks the rules, this ETH can be slashed.&nbsp;</p><p>Running a validator requires some hardware and tech skills in addition to staking 32 ETH. You must also ensure that the validator can run 24/7.&nbsp;</p><p>It’s easier to stake with dedicated protocols like <a href="https://zerion.io/blog/how-to-stake-eth-lido-vs-rocket-pool-vs-stakewise/" rel="noreferrer">Lido or Rocket Pool</a>.</p><p>We’ll show you how to find the best place for staking ETH but first, let’s explore how much you can earn.&nbsp;</p><h2 id="eth-staking-apy">ETH staking APY&nbsp;</h2><p>The APY or annual percentage yield for staking ETH is between 2% and 5%.&nbsp;</p><p>The yield is paid in ETH, and it can fluctuate depending on several factors:&nbsp;</p><ul><li>How much competition there is between validators — the more ETH is staked, the lower is the yield everyone earns&nbsp;</li><li>How many transactions there are — when the blockchain is busy (and gas is expensive), the yield is higher&nbsp;</li></ul><p>How much you earn also depends on how you stake ETH. Unless you stake ETH on your own, you will need to pay some fees to the staking protocol.&nbsp;</p><h2 id="how-to-stake-eth">How to stake ETH&nbsp;</h2><p>There are several ways to stake ETH:&nbsp;</p><ul><li><strong>Solo staking</strong> — you need 32 ETH and technical expertise to run your own validator&nbsp;</li><li><strong>Staking with a centralized exchange </strong>— you trust that the exchange or other provider will keep it safe and will not run into problems with regulators&nbsp;</li><li><strong>Staking with an onchain pool </strong>— you supply any amount of ETH, and it will be routed to a validator using smart contracts&nbsp;</li></ul><p>The easiest and safest way to stake ETH is using an onchain staking pool. That way, you do not have to trust anybody. Your ETH is securely locked in a smart contract. Only you can withdraw it. And you can do that whenever you want without anyone’s permission.&nbsp;</p><p>With most onchain staking protocols like Lido or Rocket Pool, you will also get liquid staking tokens (LSTs).
