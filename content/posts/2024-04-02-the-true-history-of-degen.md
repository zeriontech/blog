<p>Today, we venture into ancient history, all the way back to Christmas 2023. From there we will trace the origins of the DEGEN token, explore how it emerged as the largest memecoin on Base, and outgrew that status to define a category of its own. Sit down, fellow travelers, it's a long journey that cites many Farcaster posts because that's where this history was made.</p><h2 id="farcaster-native-memecoins">Farcaster-native memecoins</h2><p>DEGEN wasn’t the first <a href="https://zerion.io/base-wallet" rel="noreferrer">Base L2</a> memecoin to launch on <a href="https://zerion.io/blog/how-to-use-farcaster-and-warpcast-zerion-blog/" rel="noreferrer">Farcaster</a>, the sufficiently decentralized social network.&nbsp;</p><p>First came $POINTS, a satirical take on off-chain points by Rainbow Wallet and others. Then $WOWOW, referencing low-effort replies discouraged by Farcaster co-founder <a href="https://warpcast.com/dwr.eth" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow ugc">Dan Romero</a>. And, of course, $FARTS, because… well, why not.&nbsp;</p><p>All these new memecoins are best used with <a href="https://link.zerion.io/qGOJ1SWmSNb" rel="noreferrer"><strong>Zerion Wallet</strong></a>, which tracks all assets across all major chains.  </p><p>Those early experiments proved that Farcaster is the perfect launchpad for memecoins:&nbsp;</p><ul><li>The audience is niche and crypto-savvy&nbsp;</li><li>Onchain handles can be used to create lists for distribution</li><li>Farcaster channels are natural places to build communities&nbsp;</li></ul><p>And launching in a channel with an existing community is even better than building it from scratch.&nbsp;</p><h2 id="airdrop-1">Airdrop 1</h2><p>Christmas came early when <a href="https://warpcast.com/jacek" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow ugc"><strong>Jacek</strong></a>, the lead dev behind DEGEN, announced his new project.</p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card kg-card-hascaption"><a href="https://warpcast.com/jacek/0x8eb071f6"><img src="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/2024/04/image1.png" class="kg-image" alt="DEGEN announcement - Warpcast" loading="lazy" width="909" height="444" srcset="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/size/w600/2024/04/image1.png 600w, https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/2024/04/image1.png 909w" sizes="(min-width: 720px) 720px"></a><figcaption><span style="white-space: pre-wrap;">Source: </span><a href="https://warpcast.com/jacek/0x8eb071f6"><span style="white-space: pre-wrap;">https://warpcast.com/jacek/0x8eb071f6</span></a></figcaption></figure><p>Back then, the Farcaster community was still small, less than 2k daily active users. As the market still felt bearish, practically all of these users were committed builders and/or degens.&nbsp;</p><p>Naturally, the <a href="https://warpcast.com/~/channel/degen" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow ugc"><strong>/degen</strong></a> channel was one of the most popular hangouts on Farcaster. Launched by the <a href="https://zerion.io/blog/how-to-use-farcaster-and-warpcast-zerion-blog/" rel="noreferrer">Warpcast</a> team as one of the first channels, it was a place to discuss crypto prices and various opportunities for getting rekt.&nbsp;</p><p><strong>$DEGEN Airdrop 1</strong> (15% of the total supply of 37 billion) was allocated to Farcaster users who posted in the /degen channel, with higher allocations based on multipliers for various other actions (e.g., getting more engagement).&nbsp;The goal was to make distribution more fair, avoiding giving out disproportionate amounts to few "rich" users.</p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card kg-card-hascaption"><img src="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/2024/04/image2--1-.png" class="kg-image" alt="Degen airdrop Dune" loading="lazy" width="1556" height="690" srcset="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/size/w600/2024/04/image2--1-.png 600w, https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/size/w1000/2024/04/image2--1-.png 1000w, https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/2024/04/image2--1-.png 1556w" sizes="(min-width: 720px) 720px"><figcaption><span style="white-space: pre-wrap;">Source: </span><a href="https://dune.com/gooddata_badguy/degen-airdrop-tracking" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow ugc"><span style="white-space: pre-wrap;">https://dune.com/gooddata_badguy/degen-airdrop-tracking</span></a></figcaption></figure><p>Another 15% of the supply was dedicated to the liquidity pool. So theoretically, the price would only go to zero if <em>everybody </em>claimed and <em>everybody </em>sold — this created the floor for the price, as <a href="https://aburra.xyz/space/170/recording" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow ugc">Jacek explained on Aburro</a>.</p><p>The fair launch distribution immediately formed the core community of holders, who were indeed degens, many of them terminally onchain. They didn’t need to be sold on it. The story resonated with them. It reflected their identity. And it was a badge of honor showing that they are early adopters. It was an instant hit.</p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card kg-card-hascaption"><img src="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/2024/04/image3.png" class="kg-image" alt="screenshot of Warpcast " loading="lazy" width="909" height="444" srcset="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/size/w600/2024/04/image3.png 600w, https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/2024/04/image3.png 909w" sizes="(min-width: 720px) 720px"><figcaption><span style="white-space: pre-wrap;">Source: </span><a href="https://warpcast.com/jacek/0x92824c99"><span style="white-space: pre-wrap;">https://warpcast.com/jacek/0x92824c99</span></a></figcaption></figure><h2 id="community-based-branding">Community-based branding&nbsp;</h2><p>Apart from the name and the ticker, DEGEN launched with almost no branding.&nbsp;</p><p>Instead, the brand was built by the community from scratch.&nbsp;</p><p><a href="https://warpcast.com/0xen" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow ugc"><strong>0xen.eth</strong></a><strong>, </strong><a href="https://warpcast.com/wake" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow ugc"><strong>@wake</strong></a>, <a href="https://warpcast.com/yes2crypto.eth" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow ugc"><strong>@yes2crypto.eth</strong></a>, and other OGs played an outsized role in coining, defining, and embracing key community terms:&nbsp;</p><ul><li><strong><em>Degentlemen</em></strong> — the native name of DEGEN holders&nbsp;</li><li><strong><em>Degenerational wealth</em></strong> — as the goal of the community&nbsp;</li><li><strong><em>Top hat</em></strong> —&nbsp;as the symbol and emoji that can be added in messages and names</li></ul><p>The top hat playfully referenced the idea of being part of the new Farcaster aristocracy. Being early. Living in a different world, away from the vulgarities of Crypto Twitter. It was aspirational meme that could rally the early community.&nbsp;</p><h3 id="degentlemen-cabal">Degentlemen cabal&nbsp;</h3><p>When the Warpcast team dropped group chats as its latest feature, the DEGEN community jumped right into in.&nbsp;</p><p>@0xen swiftly created the Degentlemen groupchat. Initially limited to just 200 people, all the spots quickly filled out. The chat was the who-is-who of the Faristocracy. Even a16z’s Marc Anderseen was in it (although briefly kicked out and then reinstated).</p><p>The chat became the heart of the conversation around $DEGEN and a source of alpha on the latest developments. &nbsp;</p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card kg-card-hascaption"><img src="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/2024/04/image4.png" class="kg-image" alt="Warpcast screenshot - Degentlemen chat" loading="lazy" width="909" height="845" srcset="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/size/w600/2024/04/image4.png 600w, https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/2024/04/image4.png 909w" sizes="(min-width: 720px) 720px"><figcaption><span style="white-space: pre-wrap;">Source: </span><a href="https://warpcast.com/0xen/0xf4e4f6e2"><span style="white-space: pre-wrap;">https://warpcast.com/0xen/0xf4e4f6e2</span></a></figcaption></figure><h3 id="logo-design">Logo design&nbsp;</h3><p>As one of the first bounties, Jacek called on the community to create the logo.&nbsp;</p><p>The original placeholder logo was a poker chip with a dollar sign — something any degen would recognize. And it was purple to reflect its Farcaster-native nature.&nbsp;However, it was too generic.</p><p>In logo submissions, two traits dominated:&nbsp;</p><ul><li><strong>Top hat </strong>—&nbsp;the dominant meme</li><li><strong>Purple </strong>— the Farcaster purple to be precise, reflecting the origin of the token&nbsp;</li></ul><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card kg-card-hascaption"><img src="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/2024/04/image5.png" class="kg-image" alt="Warpcast screenshot - DEGEN logo bounty" loading="lazy" width="909" height="756" srcset="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/size/w600/2024/04/image5.png 600w, https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/2024/04/image5.png 909w" sizes="(min-width: 720px) 720px"><figcaption><span style="white-space: pre-wrap;">Source: </span><a href="https://warpcast.com/jacek/0xcf0acada"><span style="white-space: pre-wrap;">https://warpcast.com/jacek/0xcf0acada</span></a></figcaption></figure><h2 id="early-farcaster%E2%80%99s-support">Early Farcaster’s support&nbsp;</h2><p>All this $DEGEN-related activity likely affected Farcaster’s metrics — in a good way.</p><p>So Farcaster co-founder Dan Romero dropped a surprise feature: <a href="https://warpcast.com/dwr.eth/0xdf2d41a9" rel="noreferrer">the top hat reaction</a> for casts mentioning&nbsp;$DEGEN (see that hat on the next screenshot?)</p><h3 id="bountycaster">Bountycaster&nbsp;</h3><p>DEGEN started to permeate the Farcaster community beyond degenerate traders. Builders were also looking into ways to tap in that energy.&nbsp;</p><p>One early example is <a href="https://warpcast.com/bountycaster" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow ugc"><strong>Bountycaster</strong></a>, which rolled out the support for bounties paid in $DEGEN tokens.&nbsp;</p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card kg-card-hascaption"><img src="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/2024/04/image6.png" class="kg-image" alt="Warpcast screenshot - Bountycaster announcement of DEGEN support" loading="lazy" width="909" height="437" srcset="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/size/w600/2024/04/image6.png 600w, https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/2024/04/image6.png 909w" sizes="(min-width: 720px) 720px"><figcaption><span style="white-space: pre-wrap;">Source: </span><a href="https://warpcast.com/linda/0x0d41247e"><span style="white-space: pre-wrap;">https://warpcast.com/linda/0x0d41247e</span></a></figcaption></figure><h3 id="degen-bot">Degen Bot&nbsp;</h3><p>The cool thing about Farcaster is that it’s an open and (mostly) onchain — hackers can build anything they want without anyone’s permission.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</p><p>The next day after launch, <a href="https://warpcast.com/limone.eth" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow ugc"><strong>@limone.eth</strong></a> launched a Farcaster bot that tracks buys and sells of DEGEN. And then casts the trades.&nbsp;</p><p>Other users shared these casts, celebrating big buys and shaming sells. Some felt an obligation to explain their paperhand sells…</p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card kg-card-hascaption"><img src="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/2024/04/image7.png" class="kg-image" alt="Warpcast screenshot of Degenbot" loading="lazy" width="909" height="537" srcset="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/size/w600/2024/04/image7.png 600w, https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/2024/04/image7.png 909w" sizes="(min-width: 720px) 720px"><figcaption><span style="white-space: pre-wrap;">Source: </span><a href="https://warpcast.com/limone.eth/0x3b1a1cb2"><span style="white-space: pre-wrap;">https://warpcast.com/limone.eth/0x3b1a1cb2</span></a></figcaption></figure><h3 id="basepaint-day-169">Basepaint Day 169</h3><p>Collaborative painting on <a href="https://basepaint.xyz/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow ugc">Basepaint</a> was the perfect match for DEGEN’s community. So <a href="https://warpcast.com/chris24.eth" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow ugc"><strong>@chris24.eth</strong></a> proposed the theme of the day and it won.</p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><img src="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/2024/04/image8.png" class="kg-image" alt="" loading="lazy" width="1170" height="796" srcset="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/size/w600/2024/04/image8.png 600w, https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/size/w1000/2024/04/image8.png 1000w, https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/2024/04/image8.png 1170w" sizes="(min-width: 720px) 720px"></figure><p><a href="https://opensea.io/assets/base/0xba5e05cb26b78eda3a2f8e3b3814726305dcac83/169" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow ugc">Day 169</a> now has a floor price that’s significantly higher than most other ‘days’.&nbsp;With just 713 items, it's a rare onchain artifact. And a physical copy is proudly displayed in Jacek's office.</p><h2 id="liquidity-mining-rewards">Liquidity mining rewards&nbsp;</h2><p>Of course, it was not all about the memes. Any memecoin two faces:&nbsp;</p><ul><li><strong>meme</strong> — the idea, the lore, the culture around&nbsp;</li><li><strong>coin </strong>— the ticker, the liquidity, and onchain metrics.&nbsp;</li></ul><p>$DEGEN nailed both. The early liquidity mining rewards were lavish and open to anyone not just whales.&nbsp;</p><p>And because $DEGEN is on Base, an L2 with cheap transactions and easy onboarding from Coinbase, lots of new people joined the LP game on Uniswap.&nbsp;</p><p>Some of those people were doing this for the first time. They might have missed the DeFi summer due to high gas costs of deploying and managing liquidity. Now on Base anyone could add to the pool for less than a $1. Even LPing as little as $50 as an experiment made sense.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p><p>And a public leaderboard gamified the process, spurring competition among degens.&nbsp;</p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card kg-card-hascaption"><img src="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/2024/04/image9.png" class="kg-image" alt="Screenshot of the leaderboard for DEGEN liquidity mining" loading="lazy" width="1577" height="683" srcset="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/size/w600/2024/04/image9.png 600w, https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/size/w1000/2024/04/image9.png 1000w, https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/2024/04/image9.png 1577w" sizes="(min-width: 720px) 720px"><figcaption><span style="white-space: pre-wrap;">Leaderboard for DEGEN liquidity mining</span></figcaption></figure><h2 id="frames">Frames</h2><p>On January 26, Farcaster launched its latest killer feature: <a href="https://docs.farcaster.xyz/learn/what-is-farcaster/frames" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow ugc">Frames</a>, interactive apps embedded directly in the feed.</p><p><a href="https://warpcast.com/jacek/0x85d20682" rel="noreferrer">Jacek announced a bounty</a> to create a Degen frame on Farcaster. </p><p>Builders responded by creating all sorts of things for DEGEN:&nbsp;</p><ul><li>Giveaways&nbsp;</li><li>Minting NFTs with DEGEN</li><li>Games with DEGEN prizes&nbsp;</li></ul><p>And it was not limited to degens, the whole Farcaster community was excited about frames. Developers from across web3 suddenly turned to Farcaster.</p><p>Farcaster suddenly experienced the hockey stick growth — can you guess the Frames launch date on the charts?</p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card kg-width-wide kg-card-hascaption"><img src="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/2024/04/image10.png" class="kg-image" alt="Dune charts for Farcaster" loading="lazy" width="1625" height="711" srcset="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/size/w600/2024/04/image10.png 600w, https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/size/w1000/2024/04/image10.png 1000w, https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/size/w1600/2024/04/image10.png 1600w, https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/2024/04/image10.png 1625w" sizes="(min-width: 1200px) 1200px"><figcaption><span style="white-space: pre-wrap;">Source: </span><a href="https://dune.com/pixelhack/farcaster" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow ugc"><span style="white-space: pre-wrap;">https://dune.com/pixelhack/farcaster</span></a></figcaption></figure><h2 id="the-spillout-to-crypto-twitter">The Spillout to Crypto Twitter</h2><p>Along with Frames, the DEGEN price action caught the attention of Crypto Twitter.&nbsp;</p><p>In a self-reinforcing loop, the growth of Farcaster and DEGEN brought new users. Farcaster doesn’t have a token so DEGEN became the de-facto FC coin. By doing something with $DEGEN, farmers thought they could farm Farcaster.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</p><p>So somewhere around early February, the number DEGEN holders shot up along with the price.</p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card kg-width-wide"><img src="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/2024/04/image11.png" class="kg-image" alt="" loading="lazy" width="1863" height="711" srcset="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/size/w600/2024/04/image11.png 600w, https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/size/w1000/2024/04/image11.png 1000w, https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/size/w1600/2024/04/image11.png 1600w, https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/2024/04/image11.png 1863w" sizes="(min-width: 1200px) 1200px"></figure><p>&nbsp;“Killing zeros” from the DEGEN price was the name of the game. Degens, old and newly converted, rode the&nbsp;wave.</p><h2 id="integrations">Integrations</h2><p>The <a href="https://app.zerion.io/tokens/DEGEN-d590ac9c-6971-42db-b900-0bd057033ae0" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow ugc">$DEGEN price</a> action and the meme momentum (mementum?) carried on and integrations started rolling in. Degentlemen were an attractive audience for protocol and dapp builders.</p><p>One early example was <a href="https://timeswap.io/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow ugc">Timeswap</a>, which unlocked leverage (degen's poison of choice).</p><h2 id="angel-investing-round">Angel investing round&nbsp;</h2><p>In early February, a $1 million worth of ETH was swapped into $DEGEN. Turned out, that was <a href="https://warpcast.com/nick/0xecefe84b" rel="noreferrer">Nick Tomaino and his VC fund 1confirmation</a>.</p><p>Later, Jacek announced the completion of <a href="https://warpcast.com/jacek/0xe5b10763" rel="noreferrer">an angel investing round of 490 ETH</a> or around $1.5 million at the time. Coupled with the surging price of ETH and the early bull run, many LPs and early traders were cashing out their gains. The DEGEN price temporarily dropped.&nbsp;But that proved to be a short-lived dip.</p><h2 id="airdrop-2">Airdrop 2&nbsp;</h2><p>Amid all the Frames madness, Jacek announced Airdrop 2.</p><p>The second airdrop revealed the tips component: get a free daily allocation of DEGEN to tip anyone on Farcaster.&nbsp;</p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><img src="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/2024/04/image12.png" class="kg-image" alt="" loading="lazy" width="909" height="492" srcset="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/size/w600/2024/04/image12.png 600w, https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/2024/04/image12.png 909w" sizes="(min-width: 720px) 720px"></figure><p>That move spurred the flood of posts, some of them were from farmers and spammers.&nbsp;Remember, that was also the time when Crypto Twitter first learned about Farcaster. Some of those new arrivals brought their bad habits (and bot farms).</p><p>Jacek <a href="https://warpcast.com/jacek/0x8f235333" rel="noreferrer">came back with a fix</a>, banning farmers and 3,3 posts tipping for tips.&nbsp;</p><p>Eventually, the community found a way to organically tip DEGEN. Ass the price went up, daily allowances became worth substantial amounts in $ terms. It made more sense to give them out to those who truly contributed useful content.</p><p>Still, almost a <a href="https://warpcast.com/sidshekhar/0xe4a6fe4b" rel="noreferrer">third (!) of all casts now featured $DEGEN</a>.</p><h2 id="more-liquidity-aerodrome-first-cexes">More liquidity: Aerodrome, first CEXes</h2><p>The early dream of many $DEGEN holders was the Coinbase listing.</p><p>While it is yet to happen, the liquidity has diversified.&nbsp;</p><p>First, <a href="https://aerodrome.finance/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow ugc"><strong>Aerodrome</strong></a>, the Base version of Optimism’s Velodrome, brought a new source of <a href="https://warpcast.com/jacek/0x40800593" rel="noreferrer">yield and incentive rewards to LPs</a>.&nbsp;</p><p>Later in March, the first listings on centralized exchanges (CEXes) arrived:&nbsp;<em>BitMart, HTX, BingX, Poloniex...</em></p><p>DEGEN is now the most widely listed Base token. This was purely organic, and game theory suggests more CEXes will be forced to join the game to take advantage of the demand. Nobody would want to miss out on gaining new clients. &nbsp;</p><h2 id="apps-with-degen">Apps with DEGEN</h2><p>The strength and the size of DEGEN holders also made it very attractive for app builders.</p><p><a href="https://perl.xyz/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow ugc"><strong>Perl</strong></a>, a Farcaster betting game, was an early success. It was the first consumer app to use a memecoin as major part of its GTM strategy.
