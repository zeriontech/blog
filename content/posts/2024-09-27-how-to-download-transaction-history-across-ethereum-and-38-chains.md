In this post, we’ll show you how to download your Ethereum transactions as CSV files for in-depth analysis or preparing your taxes.&nbsp;</p><h2 id="how-to-export-ethereum-transactions">How to Export Ethereum Transactions</h2><p>Your Ethereum transaction history is stored on the blockchain. All your past onchain transactions are etched forever in past blocks.&nbsp;</p><p>You can access this blockchain data using:&nbsp;</p><ul><li>Blockchain explorers like <a href="https://etherscan.io/" rel="noreferrer">Etherscan</a> — free but limited in scope&nbsp;</li><li>Advanced wallets like <a href="https://link.zerion.io/6ktpLxFnSNb" rel="noreferrer">Zerion Wallet</a> —&nbsp;free or paid, flexible and easy to use&nbsp;</li><li>Specialized services like CryptoTaxCalculator — paid and built specifically for taxes&nbsp;&nbsp;</li><li>APIs like Zerion API —&nbsp;paid and fully flexible but require coding skills&nbsp;</li></ul><p>Unfortunately, older wallets like MetaMask don’t store your transaction history.&nbsp;</p><p>As a result, the most popular way of accessing and exporting MetaMask transaction history is through blockchain explorers like Etherscan.&nbsp;</p><h3 id="exporting-transactions-with-etherscan">Exporting transactions with Etherscan&nbsp;</h3><p>You can export Ethereum transaction history for any address in several steps.</p><ol><li>Open <a href="https://etherscan.io/" rel="noreferrer">Etherscan</a> and enter your address in the search
