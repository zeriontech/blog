<h4 id="amidst-the-covid-19-pandemic-market-volatility-and-fears-of-the-next-recession-here%E2%80%99s-why-you%E2%80%99re-still-better-off-putting-your-money-in-a-defi-portfolio"><em>Amidst the COVID-19 pandemic, market volatility and fears of the next recession, here’s why you’re still better off putting your money in a DeFi portfolio.</em></h4><p>Last year, we published a report that compared the returns from holding <a href="https://zerion.io/ethereum-wallet" rel="noreferrer">Ethereum</a> and Bitcoin to holding DeFi assets on Compound, Uniswap and TokenSets. We found that Compound and Uniswap offer very attractive yield opportunities for investors holding stablecoins, and TokenSet’s ETH 20-Day MACO outperformed ETH buy-and-hold in returns while significantly reducing drawdowns. This time, we take a closer look at how DeFi has performed in the first quarter of 2020.</p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card kg-width-wide kg-card-hascaption"><img src="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/max/1200/0-Rgi6EWODGO0itb_a.png" class="kg-image" alt="DeFi portfolio returns vs ETH and BTC 2019" loading="lazy" width="1200" height="397" srcset="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/size/w600/max/1200/0-Rgi6EWODGO0itb_a.png 600w, https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/size/w1000/max/1200/0-Rgi6EWODGO0itb_a.png 1000w, https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/max/1200/0-Rgi6EWODGO0itb_a.png 1200w" sizes="(min-width: 1200px) 1200px"><figcaption><span style="white-space: pre-wrap;">Summary from our 2019&nbsp;report</span></figcaption></figure><p>Over the past 6 months, the DeFi space has grown rapidly. 2020 began with a bang when the market cap of DeFi assets surpassed $1 billion – however, the excitement was short lived. In light of the Covid-19 pandemic and the subsequent economic downturn, both traditional and crypto markets have been dealt some heavy blows. DeFi has not been immune to the risk. The massive drop in Ethereum’s price a few weeks ago led to what is now known as “Crypto Black Thursday” and resulted in MakerDAO losing $4m+ in collateral. Many crypto investors are left wondering whether their assets can survive the current market volatility.</p><h3 id="does-it-still-make-sense-to-invest-in-defi">Does it still make sense to invest in DeFi?</h3><p>The answer: a resounding yes. Right now, many investors are turning to asset-backed stablecoins to weather the storm. To test this, you can check out the stable.zerion.eth wallet which was created on 21 January 2020 with 0.6 ETH (about $100 at the time). 60% of the portfolio was put into stablecoin bets on Compound (DAI and USDC) and 40% of the portfolio was put into Uniswap liquidity pools. Liquidity pools allow investors to earn a yield on their holdings through trading fees and tend to soften losses and gains. Reduced exposure to ETH price combined with income from liquidity fees may therefore be an attractive strategy for conservative ETH holders looking to earn a return while reducing risk from falls in the price of ETH.</p><p>The first graph below shows an overview from 7 March to 7 April and the second graph zooms in on the past week (7 to 13 April):</p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><img src="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/max/800/0-xDDOGEMRo6L1Lbf6.png" class="kg-image" alt="Stablecoin and liquidity pool DeFi portfolio" loading="lazy" width="800" height="387" srcset="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/size/w600/max/800/0-xDDOGEMRo6L1Lbf6.png 600w, https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/max/800/0-xDDOGEMRo6L1Lbf6.png 800w" sizes="(min-width: 720px) 720px"></figure><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card kg-card-hascaption"><img src="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/max/800/0-O4Z47I2BKPUW_rWo.png" class="kg-image" alt="Stablecoin and liquidity pool DeFi portfolio" loading="lazy" width="800" height="386" srcset="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/size/w600/max/800/0-O4Z47I2BKPUW_rWo.png 600w, https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/max/800/0-O4Z47I2BKPUW_rWo.png 800w" sizes="(min-width: 720px) 720px"><figcaption><span style="white-space: pre-wrap;">stable.zerion.eth performance</span></figcaption></figure><p>In February, the price of ETH shot up to a high of around $280, causing an increase in the value and trading activity of ETH liquidity pools. During this time, the value of the portfolio increased from $100 to $135. However, due to global fears of an economic meltdown brought on by the Coronavirus, the Dow dropped 9.99% on March 12th. Four days later on March 16th, the Dow made a record free fall of 12.93%. Crypto markets felt the impact, too. The portfolio lost most of its gains from the bull run and has now hovered consistently around the $100 mark.</p><p>Uniswap is a great tool to earn trading fees while maintaining your ETH exposure in case the market turns bullish. For investors who fear a price peak approaching but don’t want to risk selling too soon, liquidity pools like Uniswap’s ETH-DAI pair are an attractive option.</p><p>This ability to buffer market volatility in times of extreme uncertainty is a major advantage of investing in DeFi assets in general. Stablecoins are just one approach and offer short-term protection against risk. Below, we’ll take a look at the performance of a higher-value DeFi portfolio over a longer period.</p><h3 id="defi-returns-for-q1-2020">DeFi returns for Q1 2020</h3><p>For the first quarter of 2020, we analyzed the returns of:</p><ul><li>Lending DAI on Compound,</li><li>Supplying liquidity to Uniswap’s DAI-ETH pool, and</li><li>Investing in TokenSets’ ETH 20-Day and 50-Day Moving Average Crossover strategies.</li></ul><p>We also present the returns for Ethereum and Bitcoin buy-and-hold strategies for the same period.</p><p>Note:</p><ul><li>We included additional dates up to April 13th to analyze performance during the Covid-19 pandemic.</li><li>This analysis does not account for trading fees, slippage, or stablecoin price fluctuations.</li></ul><h4 id="compound-returns"><strong>Compound Returns</strong></h4><p><a href="https://compound.finance/" rel="noopener">Compound</a> is a lending protocol that allows users to pool assets which can then be borrowed after providing collateral. In the first case the investor’s portfolio value would have grown to $23,763 and in the second case, $17,522.</p><h4 id="ethereum-and-bitcoin-returns"><strong>Ethereum and Bitcoin Returns</strong></h4><p>Ethereum and Bitcoin remain highly correlated assets. However, Ethereum is starting to show more of a deviation as increased activity in the DeFi space increases demand for ETH.</p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><img src="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/max/800/0-7IXdt5dTiMN_cnon.png" class="kg-image" alt="Ethereum and Bitcoin portfolio returns chart Q1 2020" loading="lazy" width="800" height="498" srcset="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/size/w600/max/800/0-7IXdt5dTiMN_cnon.png 600w, https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/max/800/0-7IXdt5dTiMN_cnon.png 800w" sizes="(min-width: 720px) 720px"></figure><p>In our last report, we observed that both Ethereum and Bitcoin peaked at over 200% of their starting values. The drawdowns on each asset remain significantly worse than for the assets listed above — Ethereum’s lowest price resulted in a 17.38% decrease in portfolio value while Bitcoin dipped by 32.62%. At the end of the period, an Ethereum buy-and-hold strategy realized a 22.55% return and Bitcoin realized only 0.52%.</p><h4 id="summary">Summary</h4><p>The evidence is pretty clear: investing in DeFi is a smarter way to manage your portfolio. Overall, automated trading strategies such as the ETH 20-Day MACO and ETH 50-Day MACO offered 137.63% and 75.22% returns respectively. Lending strategies on Compound and liquidity pools on Uniswap experienced drawdowns almost 20x lower than those experienced from holding ETH or BTC. In addition, as we’ve seen, stablecoin liquidity pools can be a lifeline in times of extreme uncertainty.</p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card kg-width-wide"><img src="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/max/1200/0-TO1-kHRrPRxYibxa.png" class="kg-image" alt="Summary DeFi portfolio returns Q1 2020" loading="lazy" width="1200" height="315" srcset="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/size/w600/max/1200/0-TO1-kHRrPRxYibxa.png 600w, https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/size/w1000/max/1200/0-TO1-kHRrPRxYibxa.png 1000w, https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/max/1200/0-TO1-kHRrPRxYibxa.png 1200w" sizes="(min-width: 1200px) 1200px"></figure><p>These results also say something broader about the evolution of the DeFi ecosystem. No market has been immune to the volatility that our world is now experiencing on all fronts – economic, political and social – but the safety net offered by assets designed to survive these conditions is promising.
