<p>You probably heard about music NFTs as the next hot thing.</p><p>But where to find them? Who are the leading Web3 music artists and collectors? And how to enjoy these MP3s once you mint a few of them?</p><p>That’s where <a href="https://www.oohlala.xyz/">Oohlala</a> comes in.</p><p>In this post, we’ll show you how Oohlala aggregates Web3 music and how it can help you get started with music NFTs.</p><div class="kg-card kg-callout-card kg-callout-card-blue"><div class="kg-callout-emoji">💡</div><div class="kg-callout-text">First, install <a href="https://link.zerion.io/LSTER0StMyb" rel="noreferrer">Zerion Wallet</a> — it's the perfect companion for web3 music. </div></div><h2 id="what-is-web3-music">What is Web3 music?</h2><p>Let’s make sure we’re on the same page.</p><p>Web3 music is the space that’s emerging around music NFTs or non-fungible tokens with audio as the main asset.</p><p>Just like with regular JPEG NFTs, you can usually enjoy music NFTs without owning them. Anyone can listen to the file and, yes they can even right-click-save it.</p><p>They key idea is ownership. Like other NFTs, Web3 music creates new relationships between artists who <a href="https://zerion.io/blog/how-to-make-and-sell-music-nft/">sell music NFTs</a> and their audiences. Instead of buying things like T-shirts or vinyl records, you can directly support your favorite artists. You can think of music NFTs as <a href="https://zerion.mirror.xyz/qq-ljlAe_iLitNSkC-9Z7H735h1AjQs0-3AFbEJmxH4">the digital version of vinyl</a>.</p><p>Web3 music also creates new dynamics for exploring fan communities. You can find other fans by checking out who else mints your favorite artists. Then you can see what else they collect.</p><p>You can do this by browsing addresses with <a href="https://link.zerion.io/LSTER0StMyb" rel="noreferrer">Zerion Wallet</a>. For example, here is the <a href="https://app.zerion.io/0x7241dddec3a6af367882eaf9651b87e1c7549dff/nfts">Stani.lens</a> address, owned by Stani Koulechov, the founder of Aave and Lens.</p><p>However, things get tricky when an address has hundreds of different NFTs. Music NFT gems can easily get lost. And even when you find them, you can’t keep them or organize them.</p><p>Luckily, Oohlala.xyz can help with this and much more.</p><h2 id="what-is-oohlala">What is Oohlala?</h2><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><img src="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/2023/04/Screenshot-2023-04-06-at-15.20.52.png" class="kg-image" alt="" loading="lazy" width="2000" height="956" srcset="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/size/w600/2023/04/Screenshot-2023-04-06-at-15.20.52.png 600w, https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/size/w1000/2023/04/Screenshot-2023-04-06-at-15.20.52.png 1000w, https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/size/w1600/2023/04/Screenshot-2023-04-06-at-15.20.52.png 1600w, https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/size/w2400/2023/04/Screenshot-2023-04-06-at-15.20.52.png 2400w" sizes="(min-width: 720px) 720px"></figure><p>Oohlala is a Web3 music player &amp; aggregator that seeks to improve the music NFT experience.</p><p>The Web3 music space is fragmented, with no dominant platform or standard for music NFTs. Not all wallets also properly display music NFTs or let you play the audio (<a href="https://link.zerion.io/LSTER0StMyb" rel="noreferrer">Zerion Wallet</a> does!).</p><p>The Oohlala team have done all the heavy lifting of integrating different Web3 music platforms and music NFT collections to display them all in one place.</p><h3 id="creating-a-profile">Creating a profile</h3><p>If you already own some music NFTs, just connect your wallet to Oohlala, and it will show all your music from over 18 platforms and collections.</p><p>You can then customize your profile, adding a description and avatar. There is also an option to verify your profile on Twitter.</p><p>While you can listen to music on Oohlala without creating a profile, it’s useful for saving tracks and adding them to playlists.</p><h3 id="queue-and-playlists">Queue and Playlists</h3><p>You can add any track you see on Oohlala — even you don’t own that music NFT — to the queue or playlists. This works similarly to Spotify or other music streaming platforms.</p><h3 id="likes">Likes</h3><p>On Oohlala, you can also like an individual music NFT, an artist, or a playlist.</p><p>All these likes then show up in your Library. It’s a great way to put everything in one place before neatly arranging music NFTs in playlists or placing bids on marketplaces.</p><h3 id="listening-to-web3-music-on-mobile">Listening to Web3 music on mobile!</h3><p>Oohlala also has apps for <a href="https://apps.apple.com/app/ooh-la-la-player/id1636453776">iOS</a> and <a href="https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=xyz.spiderville.oohlala">Android</a> — this means you can listen to your music NFTs on the go.</p><p>Sadly, on iPhone, the mobile app doesn’t let you mint new music NFTs due to Apple’s rules.</p><p>However, with <a href="https://link.zerion.io/LSTER0StMyb" rel="noreferrer">Zerion Wallet</a>, you can simply open oohlala.xyz in your <a href="https://zerion.io/blog/what-is-dapp-browser/">Zerion’s dapp browser</a> and mint anything you want.</p><p>But first, you need to find interesting tracks.</p><h2 id="how-to-find-music-nfts">How to find music NFTs</h2><p>Oohlala is also great for exploring Web3 music and finding interesting artists and tracks.</p><p>Here are at least three ways to find music NFT to add to your collection.</p><h3 id="homepage">Homepage</h3><p>On its homepage, Oohlala curates interesting tracks, open mints, playlists by other artists and the community.</p><p>Many of the music NFT mints are free or have a very small fee. And then you explore what else those collectors hold.</p><p>Whenever you find something interesting, click ‘like’ and save it to your library.</p><p>Now that you know what Oohlala is and how it works, it’s time to set up your profile.</p><h2 id="create-your-oohlala-profile">Create your Oohlala profile</h2><p>If you already have <a href="https://link.zerion.io/LSTER0StMyb" rel="noreferrer">Zerion Wallet</a>, it’s easy to set up a profile on Oohlala.</p><ol><li>Download Oohlala for <a href="https://apps.apple.com/app/ooh-la-la-player/id1636453776">iOS</a> or <a href="https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=xyz.spiderville.oohlala">Android</a></li><li>Tap on 'Profile' and then ‘Connect wallet’</li><li>Choose Zerion Wallet – you'll be redirected to a page with a 'Open Zerion Wallet' button</li><li>In the <a href="https://link.zerion.io/LSTER0StMyb">Zerion Wallet</a> app, tap 'Connect' </li><li>Go back to the Oohlala mobile app — tap 'Verify'</li><li>Again, you'll be directed to the Zerion Wallet app, where you need to sign a message, confirming that it's your address</li></ol><p>That’s it!
