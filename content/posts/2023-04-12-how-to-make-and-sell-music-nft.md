In contrast, each ETH token is just like another. But each NFT is one-of-a-kind and traceable, even if has a certain number of copies.</p><p>Speaking of copies, NFTs could be:</p><ul><li><strong>1 of 1 or 1/1 </strong>— each NFT exists as a single edition, there is just one item in the collection;</li><li><strong>Limited editions</strong> — for example, 1 of 500 or 1/500, where each of the 500 NFTs in the collection is the same but has a unique number that identifies it;</li><li><strong>Open editions</strong> — usually, done as time-limited drops, where an unlimited number of copies can be minted (i.e., created) during a specific period (a day, a week, etc.);</li><li><strong>Drops</strong> — often a collection of 10,000, where each NFT has some unique attributes and media, making some items rarer than others. These attributes could be assigned randomly or based on some rules in the smart contract.</li></ul><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card kg-width-wide kg-card-hascaption"><img src="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/2023/03/6--1-.png" class="kg-image" alt="A screenshot of a music NFT by valentina cy at Sound.xyz" loading="lazy" width="2000" height="942" srcset="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/size/w600/2023/03/6--1-.png 600w, https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/size/w1000/2023/03/6--1-.png 1000w, https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/size/w1600/2023/03/6--1-.png 1600w, https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/size/w2400/2023/03/6--1-.png 2400w" sizes="(min-width: 1200px) 1200px"><figcaption><span style="white-space: pre-wrap;">An example of a </span><a href="https://www.sound.xyz/valentinacy/bad"><span style="white-space: pre-wrap;">music NFT by valentina cy at Sound.xyz</span></a></figcaption></figure><p>A <strong><em>smart contract</em></strong> is a program on the blockchain that defines how the NFT works. Don’t worry, you don’t need to code — most NFT platforms handle that.</p><p>The smart contract can also have resale royalty and splits. The <strong><em>splits</em></strong> mean you share revenue with other addresses (for example, collab partners or producers). The <strong><em>resale royalty</em></strong> gives you a cut every time some trades the NFT on the secondary market.</p><p>We’ll look into details later, for now, it’s more important to understand who and why buys music NFTs.</p><h2 id="why-do-people-buy-music-nfts">Why do people buy music NFTs?</h2><p>You usually don’t need to own a music NFT to listen to the track.</p><p>Instead, people buy music NFTs mainly for two reasons: to support the recording artist and to speculate on the future market value of the NFT.</p><figure class="kg-card kg-embed-card"><blockquote class="twitter-tweet"><p lang="en" dir="ltr">Collecting 1 Music NFT for 0.005 ETH pays the same as 2500 streams.<br><br>And you get a digital vinyl to show for it.</p>— Coopahtroopa 🔥_🔥 (@Cooopahtroopa) <a href="https://twitter.com/Cooopahtroopa/status/1643489307754151938?ref_src=twsrc%5Etfw">April 5, 2023</a></blockquote>

Nearly all wallets, exchanges, and platforms support Ethereum and its token ETH (used to pay for transactions). Ethereum has over 220 million unique addresses (i.e., accounts) and about 500,000 unique daily active addresses.</p><p>The main problem with Ethereum is the high fees for transactions. Minting an NFT could cost $5-10 or even hundreds of dollars, depending on how busy the network is at the time of minting.</p><p>That’s where Polygon comes in.</p><p><strong>Polygon</strong> is another blockchain technology that is faster and cheaper than Ethereum. Polygon works like Ethereum, except you need MATIC, its native token, to pay for transactions. Polygon has almost the same number of unique addresses and over 400,000 unique daily addresses.</p><p>Minting an NFT on Polygon is cheap and usually costs less than $1.</p><p>Overall, more wallets, platforms, and services support Ethereum, but it’s cheaper to get started on Polygon.</p><h2 id="where-to-sell-music-nfts-main-platforms">Where to sell music NFTs: main platforms</h2><p>You can release and sell your music as an NFT drop on two types of platforms: permissionless and curated.</p><p><strong>Permissionless platforms</strong> let anybody create and sell any kind of NFTs, including music.
