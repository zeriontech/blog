<p>Polygon, recently made headlines when Meta’s Instagram, Stripe, and Adidas announced their plans to partner with the network on a range of upcoming NFT and Web3 projects.</p><p>Despite these endorsements, many people still don’t know much about Polygon. While there is hardly a shortage of guides to Polygon, most of them are too heavy with blockchain jargon and tech details. Many crypto exchanges offer MATIC, the Polygon network’s native cryptocurrency, but don’t offer many options to use it beyond trading and basic staking use cases.</p><p>This post aims to fix this. We explain what Polygon is in simple terms and then dive into how you can use it to trade, stake, and earn yield with <a href="https://link.zerion.io/pW3T8UTWyqb">Zerion Wallet</a>.</p><h2 id="what-is-polygon">What is Polygon?</h2><p>Polygon is a scaling platform for Ethereum.</p><p>Developers can use existing Ethereum tools and frameworks to build apps on Polygon with faster transactions and lower fees.</p><p>For users, Polygon offers lower fees and faster transactions decreasing the barriers to entry for people who aren’t crypto whales so they can enjoy <a href="https://zerion.io/blog/how-to-invest-in-defi/">DeFi</a>, <a href="https://zerion.io/blog/how-to-make-buy-and-sell-nfts/">NFTs</a>, and the rest of Web3 with Polygon.</p><p>For example, some of today’s top play-to-earn games are on the Polygon network. Also, many artists choose to mint <a href="https://zerion.io/blog/polygon-nfts/">NFTs on Polygon</a>. And major DeFi protocols have versions on Polygon because it’s very easy to port smart contracts from the Ethereum mainnent.</p><p>Before exploring what you can do on Polygon, let’s quickly review how its technology has evolved.</p><h3 id="polygon-history">Polygon history</h3><p>Originally known as Matic Network, the network was launched in October 2017. The name of Polygon’s native cryptocurrency <a href="https://app.zerion.io/explore/asset/matic-0x7d1afa7b718fb893db30a3abc0cfc608aacfebb0?utm_medium=blog&amp;utm_source=article&amp;utm_campaign=what_is_polygon&amp;utm_content=matic">MATIC</a> is from that era.</p><p>The first version of Matic was a set of <a href="https://ethereum.org/en/developers/docs/scaling/plasma/#:~:text=A%20plasma%20chain%20is%20a,copies%20of%20the%20Ethereum%20Mainnet.">Plasma chains</a> that connected to the mainnet Ethereum.</p><p>The Matic team continued building during both the 2017 bull run and the 2018 bear market. And in 2019, towards the end of the crypto winter, Matic completed a $5,600,000 token sale through Binance Launchpad.</p><p>In 2021, Matic rebranded as Polygon to reflect its expanding range of scalability solutions.</p><h3 id="polygon%E2%80%99s-technology">Polygon’s technology</h3><p>Today, Polygon is more than just an eponymous Proof-of-Stake network.</p><p>It's now a flexible framework that lets developers build and connect Plasma chains, Optimistic Rollups, zkRollups, Validium as well as Polygon Proof of Stake.</p><p>Most people and apps use Polygon Proof of Stake Chain.</p><p>This is an Ethereum sidechain, secured by its own Proof of Stake algorithm. The sidechain (aka Plasma chain) has its own validators and also uses checkpoint nodes that regularly submit fraud proofs to the Ethereum mainnet, which acts as the root chain. This leverages Ethereum for increased security.</p><p>In January, Polygon PoS network hit a record of over 557,000 active addresses.</p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card kg-card-hascaption"><img src="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/2022/06/Screenshot-2022-06-03-at-15.42.42.png" class="kg-image" alt="Active addresses on Polygon" loading="lazy" width="1712" height="1206" srcset="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/size/w600/2022/06/Screenshot-2022-06-03-at-15.42.42.png 600w, https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/size/w1000/2022/06/Screenshot-2022-06-03-at-15.42.42.png 1000w, https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/size/w1600/2022/06/Screenshot-2022-06-03-at-15.42.42.png 1600w, https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/2022/06/Screenshot-2022-06-03-at-15.42.42.png 1712w" sizes="(min-width: 720px) 720px"><figcaption><span style="white-space: pre-wrap;">Active addresses on Polygon</span></figcaption></figure><h3 id="matic-vs-eth">MATIC vs ETH</h3><p>Just like ETH for Ethereum, MATIC is the native cryptocurrency of the Polygon network.</p><p>MATIC is used to pay for gas for all transactions on Polygon. It could also be staked to secure the network (more on that below).</p><h3 id="what-makes-polygon-different">What makes Polygon different?</h3><p>Like other scaling solutions, Polygon offers faster transactions with lower fees.</p><p>The main differences are in what Polygon offers to developers building on top of the protocol:</p><ul><li><strong>Established track record: </strong>Polygon POS mainnet is live since June 2020.</li><li><strong>User adoption:</strong> Polygon has over 140 million unique addresses and had a record of over 500,000 unique active addresses.</li><li><strong>Developer community:</strong> over <a href="https://blog.polygon.technology/polygon-hit-19000-dapps-milestone-sixfold-growth-in-half-a-year/">19,000 dapps</a> are live on Polygon.</li></ul><p>Coupled with full compatibility with Ethereum dev tooling, this makes Polygon an attractive network to build on.</p><h2 id="polygon-ecosystem">Polygon ecosystem</h2><p>Today, Polygon has one of the most developed ecosystems among all scaling solutions.</p><p>With a non-custodial wallet for Polygon, you can trade, stake, earn a yield, join play-to-earn games, and more.</p><p>Let’s explore these opportunities in detail.</p><h3 id="staking-matic">Staking MATIC</h3><p>Because the main Polygon network is Proof-of-Stake, you can stake <a href="https://app.zerion.io/explore/asset/matic-0x7d1afa7b718fb893db30a3abc0cfc608aacfebb0?utm_medium=blog&amp;utm_source=article&amp;utm_campaign=what_is_polygon&amp;utm_content=matic">MATIC</a> to secure the network and earn rewards.</p><p>However, MATIC must be staked on the Ethereum network, which can be expensive.</p><p>There are two main options for staking MATIC:</p><ul><li><strong>Centralized exchanges:</strong> Many exchanges that list MATIC also make it easy to stake the tokens without paying gas fees.
