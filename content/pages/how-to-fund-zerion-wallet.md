<p>You can’t do much with an empty wallet.</p><p>In this short article, we’ll show how to fund your new <a href="https://zerion.io/">Zerion Wallet</a>. You have three main options: buy with a card, withdraw from an exchange, and receive crypto from someone.</p><h2 id="buy-eth-with-a-card-in-zerion-wallet">Buy ETH with a card in Zerion Wallet</h2><p>The easiest way to fund your wallet is to simply buy some crypto with a bank card.</p><p>Whatever you plan to do with your crypto wallet, you’ll need some coins to pay for gas fees: ETH for Ethereum, MATIC for Polygon, BNB for Binance Smart Chain and so on.</p><p>Zerion partnered with several payment providers that serve hundreds of countries and offer dozens of crypto:</p><ul><li><strong>Moonpay: </strong>credit/debit cards, Apple Pay, Google Pay, SEPA and SEPA Instant transfers, Faster Payments Service</li><li><strong>Ramp: </strong>debit cards, SEPA transfers, and Faster Payments Service</li><li><strong><strong><strong>Mercuryo: </strong>Visa and MasterCard cards, Apple Pay, SEPA transfers</strong></strong></li></ul><p>Depending on your country, you might need to provide some personal information to meet the KYC requirements.</p><p><strong>1. Open your <a href="https://link.zerion.io/B9dLzjB7Qtb">Zerion Wallet app</a> and select the wallet</strong></p><p><strong>2. Click the ‘Buy Crypto’ banner</strong></p><p>If for some reason you don’t see the banner, click the three dots and then ‘Buy Crypto’.</p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><img src="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/2022/10/screely-1664894282425.png" class="kg-image" alt loading="lazy" width="1128" height="525" srcset="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/size/w600/2022/10/screely-1664894282425.png 600w, https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/size/w1000/2022/10/screely-1664894282425.png 1000w, https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/2022/10/screely-1664894282425.png 1128w" sizes="(min-width: 720px) 720px"></figure><p><strong>3. Select one of the service providers</strong></p><p>If one of them doesn't support your country or preferred payment method, just try another one.</p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><img src="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/2022/10/screely-1664893897131.png" class="kg-image" alt loading="lazy" width="1128" height="686" srcset="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/size/w600/2022/10/screely-1664893897131.png 600w, https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/size/w1000/2022/10/screely-1664893897131.png 1000w, https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/2022/10/screely-1664893897131.png 1128w" sizes="(min-width: 720px) 720px"></figure><p><strong>4. Follow the instructions for the provider</strong></p><p>Note: if some token that you want to buy isn’t available with that provider, you can just buy ETH and later trade it for any other asset at the best rate with <a href="https://zerion.io/blog/how-does-trading-aggregation-work-on-zerion/">Zerion's swap</a>.</p><h2 id="receive-crypto-from-coinbase-or-any-other-exchange">Receive crypto from Coinbase or any other exchange</h2><p>You can also buy crypto on any centralized exchange like Coinbase or Binance and then withdraw.</p><p>Here’s what you need to withdraw crypto to your Zerion Wallet:</p><p><strong>1. Open your <a href="https://link.zerion.io/B9dLzjB7Qtb">Zerion Wallet</a></strong></p><p><strong>2. Tap ‘Receive’ and copy the address</strong></p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><img src="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/2022/10/screely-1664894580801.png" class="kg-image" alt loading="lazy" width="1128" height="524" srcset="https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/size/w600/2022/10/screely-1664894580801.png 600w, https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/size/w1000/2022/10/screely-1664894580801.png 1000w, https://zerion.io/blog/content/images/2022/10/screely-1664894580801.png 1128w" sizes="(min-width: 720px) 720px"></figure><p><strong>3. Login into your exchange and select withdraw, enter your Zerion address as the destination</strong></p><p>You can use your Zerion address to send crypto via any of the supported networks, including:</p><ul><li>Ethereum</li><li>Polygon</li><li>Binance Smart Chain aka BNB Smart Chain</li><li>Fantom</li><li>Avalanche (C-Chain)</li><li>Arbitrum</li><li>Optimism</li><li>Gnosis Chain</li></ul><p>The tokens you send will appear in your wallet on your selected network. All networks enabled by default and you don’t need to do anything to set them up.</p><h2 id="receive-crypto-from-anyone-on-any-network">Receive crypto from anyone, on any network</h2><p>To receive crypto anyone, anywhere (including your other wallet) just follow these same steps:</p><ol><li><strong>Open your <a href="https://link.zerion.io/B9dLzjB7Qtb">Zerion Wallet</a></strong></li><li><strong>Tap ‘Receive’ and copy the address</strong></li><li><strong>Share this address with the person who will send your crypto</strong></li></ol><p>Again, you can use the same wallet address for any supported network.</p><h3 id="create-zerion-wallet"><a href="https://link.zerion.io/B9dLzjB7Qtb">Create Zerion Wallet</a></h3>
